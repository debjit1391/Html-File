Building Public Education
Systems that refuse to
let children down

We are a systems change
and advisory organization.

In our two-fold approach to change, we build the capacity of government system leaders and we influence system processes. In partnership with the local and state education bodies, we co-create programs that shift teaching-learning practices and improve student learning and well-being. This work coupled with our research and advisory support to influence policies, we hope to create effective public education systems in India.

What do we do?

The problems
we see

The problems in the education system are only the tip of the iceberg.

Learning quality of ~130
million low-income children

Only 28% of children in grade 3 can perform subtraction; only 50% of the students in grade 2 can read grade 2 text. Source: ASER 2018 Report

Teaching quality of 5
million teachers is poor

84% teachers struggle with delivering education through digital mediums Source: LFE Oxfam 2020

The problems
we solve

We solve systemic problems with systemic solutions.

Capacity gap of system leaders to design & implement teacher support programs

The Academic bodies (SCERT & DIETs) on average have 30% vacancy and 40% of existing staff are less than qualified. Source: 2012 CSTTE Report

Around 40% of school support staff (Cluster officers) visit schools less than once a month. Source: UNICEF survey - Maharashtra 2016

Institutional capacity gap of governments to drive quality education programs at scale

0/30 states are in the first two levels (L-1 & L-2), and 85% of states are L-5 and below in the Performance Grade Index Report. Source: MHRD-PGI Report 2018-19

28/30 States have a cumulative score of 300/360 or less, in the domain of governance processes Source: MHRD-PGI Report 2018-19

Our Pathways To Impact

Our Story

LFE began with a small team and an impactful realisation:
No single private enterprise or consortium could equitably empower 250 million school-going children with an excellent education, and therefore, it is critical to strengthen the existing public education system. Half a decade later, it is now a full-fledged organisation with a dedicated team and the same core purpose.

About us

Founded by Meghna Chawla in 2015, Foster & Forge Foundation is a non-profit, Section 8 company seeking to transform the lives of millions of school children by bolstering their education with the relevant life skills to succeed in school and beyond.
